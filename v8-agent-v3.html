Frequently Asked Questions

What is jo and how does it work?

jo is a local AI assistant for Mac that indexes your notes, photos, emails, and files. It runs entirely on your computer. Think of it as total recall for everything on your Mac.

How does jo know my stuff instantly?

jo connects directly to Notes, Photos, Mail, and your files, the same way Apple apps talk to each other. It indexes everything locally, so it's ready to help the moment you install it.

Does my data ever leave my Mac?

No. jo runs 100% on your computer. Your notes, photos, and emails never get uploaded anywhere. Nothing is shared. Nothing leaves your machine. Ever.

How is jo different from Apple's search or Siri?

Apple search matches keywords. jo understands meaning. Ask for "that email about the budget issue" and jo finds it, even if you never used the word "budget." It's like if Siri could actually find your stuff.

How does jo pricing work?

No subscription. Simple queries (finding a note, searching photos) cost ~1 cent. Complex queries that synthesize multiple sources cost ~5 cents. Most users spend $5-10/month.

Which Mac do I need for jo?

Any Mac with an M-series chip (M1, M2, M3, M4) and 16GB of memory. That's most Macs from 2020 on. The M-series Neural Engine makes local AI fast and efficient.

Will jo slow down my Mac?

You won't notice it. jo uses dedicated AI hardware (the Neural Engine) that normally sits idle, so it won't slow down anything else you're doing.

Is jo free to use?

The jo app is free to download. You only pay for queries on a per-use basis with no subscriptions. Most users spend less than $8/month, and simple queries cost just pennies.

Can jo summarize my WhatsApp and Telegram chats?

Yes. jo connects to iMessage, WhatsApp, and Telegram on your Mac. Ask "catch me up on the family chat" and jo summarizes the key points, decisions, and anything that needs your attention. All processing happens locally.

How is jo different from running my own AI agent?

Self-hosting AI agents means buying a Mac mini, renting a VPS, configuring SSH, managing API keys, and debugging at midnight. jo gives you the same capabilities with none of the infrastructure. Drag it to Applications. You're done.
